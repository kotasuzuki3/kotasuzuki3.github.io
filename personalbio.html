About Me

Hello, my name is Kota Suzuki and I am a junior at Northwestern University studying journalism, data science and statistics. As a Japanese-American and ESL student, I walked a very STEM-heavy path, only recently becoming interested in storytelling and journalism in high school. As such, my passions lie in the intersection between data and the humanities — trying to bridge this gap and exploring any and all opportunities where these disciplines converge.

Throughout my time as a student, I had the opportunity to engage in valuable endeavors and projects including the development of the SAY THEIR NAMES map for NON:op Open Opera Works, the creation of a chatbot at the University of Chicago's Data Science Institute and the founding of the Data Visualization Desk at The Daily Northwestern. Through these opportunities, I have gained experience in Python, SQL, Tableau and other coding and visualization tools. As a data scientist with a heavy journalism background, I feel I work best in team-oriented environments where I can employ both my technical proficiencies and communication skills.

Feel free to contact me if you have any questions.
